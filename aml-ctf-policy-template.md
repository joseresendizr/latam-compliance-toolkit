# AML/CTF Policy Template
## Anti-Money Laundering & Counter-Terrorism Financing Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Applicable jurisdiction:** Mexico (LFPIORPI, CNBV regulations)  
**Review cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose and scope

This policy establishes the minimum standards for the prevention, detection, and reporting of money laundering and terrorism financing activities. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties operating on behalf of the organization.

---

## 2. Regulatory framework

This policy is designed in compliance with:

- Ley Federal para la Prevención e Identificación de Operaciones con Recursos de Procedencia Ilícita (LFPIORPI)
- Disposiciones de carácter general aplicables a las instituciones de crédito (CNBV)
- FATF/GAFI Recommendations (40 Recommendations)
- Applicable Banxico circulars

---

## 3. Key definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Money laundering | The process of making illegally obtained funds appear legitimate |
| Terrorism financing | Providing financial support for terrorist activities |
| Suspicious activity | Any transaction or behavior that deviates from expected patterns |
| PEP | Politically Exposed Person — a person holding a prominent public function |
| SAR | Suspicious Activity Report filed with the FIU (UIF) |
| UIF | Unidad de Inteligencia Financiera — Mexico's Financial Intelligence Unit |

## 4. Risk-based approach

### 4.1 Customer risk classification

| Risk level | Criteria | Due diligence required |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Domestic individual, known source of funds, low transaction volume | Simplified KYC |
| Medium | SME, foreign national, moderate transaction volume | Standard KYC |
| High | PEP, cash-intensive business, high transaction volume, high-risk jurisdiction | Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) |

### 4.2 Product and channel risk

Assess risk by product type:
- Digital wallets — Medium to High
- Wire transfers — Medium to High
- Lending products — Medium
- Card payments — Low to Medium

## 5. Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements

### 5.1 Individual customers (B2C)

**Minimum required documentation:**
- [ ] Official government-issued ID (INE, passport)
- [ ] Proof of address (not older than 3 months)
- [ ] RFC (Registro Federal de Contribuyentes)
- [ ] CURP
- [ ] Source of funds declaration (for high-risk profiles)

### 5.2 Business customers (B2B)

**Minimum required documentation:**
- [ ] Acta constitutiva (articles of incorporation)
- [ ] RFC empresarial
- [ ] Comprobante de domicilio fiscal
- [ ] Identification of beneficial owners (>25% ownership)
- [ ] Powers of attorney for authorized representatives
- [ ] Financial statements (for high-risk or high-volume clients)

## 6. Transaction monitoring

### 6.1 Monitoring thresholds

Per LFPIORPI, the following transaction types trigger mandatory reporting to the UIF:

| Transaction type | Reporting threshold |
|---|---|
| Cash transactions | MXN $600,000 (approx. USD $30,000) |
| Wire transfers | MXN $600,000 |
| Purchases with cash | MXN $100,000 (real estate, vehicles, jewelry) |

### 6.2 Red flags — individual indicators

- Unusual cash deposits inconsistent with customer profile
- Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds (structuring)
- Rapid movement of funds in and out of accounts
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions (FATF grey/black list)
- Customer reluctance to provide identification or source of funds

### 6.3 Red flags — business indicators

- Shell company structures with no clear business purpose
- Transactions inconsistent with stated business activity
- Payments to/from unrelated third parties
- Sudden significant increase in transaction volume

## 7. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

### 7.1 Internal escalation process

```
Employee identifies suspicious activity
        ↓
Documents observations in internal incident log
        ↓
Escalates to Compliance Officer within 24 hours
        ↓
Compliance Officer reviews and assesses
        ↓
If confirmed → files SAR with UIF within 30 days
        ↓
Case documented and archived for 10 years
```

### 7.2 Tipping-off prohibition

Employees must NOT inform the customer or any third party that a SAR has been filed or that they are under investigation. Violation of this rule may constitute a criminal offense.

---

## 8. Record keeping

All KYC documentation and transaction records must be retained for a minimum of **10 years** from the date of the transaction or the end of the business relationship, whichever is later.

Storage requirements:
- Secure, access-controlled digital repository
- Backup copies maintained in separate location
- Accessible to regulators upon request within 48 hours

---

## 10. Compliance officer responsibilities

The designated Compliance Officer is responsible for:

- [ ] Overseeing day-to-day AML/CTF program operations
- [ ] Filing SARs with the UIF
- [ ] Maintaining and updating this policy
- [ ] Coordinating regulatory examinations
- [ ] Reporting to senior management and board on AML risk
- [ ] Managing the AML training program

---

## 11. Sanctions screening

All customers and transactions must be screened against:

- OFAC SDN List (US)
- UN Security Council Sanctions List
- EU Consolidated Sanctions List
- Mexican government lists (SAT, PGR)
- PEP databases

Screening must occur at onboarding and on an ongoing basis (minimum monthly re-screening for existing customers).

---

## 12. Policy violations

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Referral to law enforcement authorities
- Personal criminal liability under LFPIORPI
